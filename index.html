About

Trans-Canada Hyperloop is a Hyperloop route design competition based in the Ottawa Valley. The goal is to use the Hyperloop concept to educate teams about transportation planning and infrastructure design, effective communication, and the ways in which innovation can change lives everywhere.

The Hyperloop is a concept for a supersonic vacuum tube train, faster than a plane yet as easy as a bus. We believe that the future of Canada's transportation is in Hyperloop, and to that end we seek to inspire a new generation of what lies in their future. Through sponsors like Waterloop and Transpod, students will be able to reach out toCanada's leading experts in the field.

The competition will have its initial kickoff on January 15th, 2018 at high schools in Deep River and Arnprior. These kickoffs will include presentation of the competition along with presentations from Waterloop and Transpod. Once students have registered on this website, the competition will last for approximately 2 months ending on March 24th, 2018, and will be primarily coordinated through the Slack communication tool.

Through accomplishing specific goals (to be set out in the competition kickoff), teams will earn points toward a total. Over the course of two months, they will develop their idea and come up with a pitch. The competition will end with each team presenting their work in front of a panel of experts, who will determine the final winners based on presentation quality and points earned. The first prize team will be awarded $1000, and other great prizes will be available for the second and third place teams. Additionally, finalists will be invited to present at the Options Trades Event to be held at the Pembroke Memorial Centre in May 2018.

After the competition ends, we hope to receive feedback from students and generate enough interest to develop the competition throughout Canada.
